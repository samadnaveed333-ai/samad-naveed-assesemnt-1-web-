🍪 Classic Chocolate Chip Cookies

Soft, chewy, and loaded with chocolate — baked with love!

About This Recipe

These classic chocolate chip cookies have been perfected over generations. With crispy edges, a gooey centre, and pockets of melted chocolate throughout, they are the ultimate comfort bake. Whether you're making them for a bake sale, a rainy afternoon, or simply because you deserve a treat — this recipe never fails.

🍪 Did You Know? The chocolate chip cookie was invented by accident in 1938 by Ruth Wakefield, who added broken chocolate bar pieces to her cookie dough expecting them to melt — they didn't, and a legend was born!

Ingredients

  • 2 ¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, softened
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • ¾ cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 cups chocolate chips
  • Optional: ½ cup chopped walnuts

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 190°C (375°F) and line two baking trays with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.
  3. Beat softened butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until light and fluffy (about 3 minutes).
  4. Add eggs one at a time, beating well after each. Mix in the vanilla extract.
  5. Gradually stir in the flour mixture until just combined — do not overmix.
  6. Fold in the chocolate chips (and walnuts if using).
  7. Drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto trays, spacing them about 5 cm apart.
  8. Bake for 9–11 minutes until edges are golden but centres look slightly underdone.
  9. Cool on the tray for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack.
  10. Serve warm with a glass of cold milk and enjoy!

Storage Tips

Store baked cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 5 days. You can also freeze the raw cookie dough balls on a tray, then transfer to a zip-lock bag and freeze for up to 3 months. Bake from frozen, adding 2–3 extra minutes to the bake time.
